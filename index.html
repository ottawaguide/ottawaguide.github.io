TOH Cancer Centre Travel Guide

Your cancer journey will take you from your home in Nunavut or Baffin Islands to Ottawa. The Ottawa Hospital is there for you; we take a holistic approach to supporting you through your travel and treatment. This website was made to provide valuble information for those who are preparing to travel to Ottawa for treatment.

Preparation before leaving home

Sometimes, there is little warning of when exactly you will be travelling. Also, it is difficult to know exactly how long you will be away from home.
  1. 1

    Ask Someone To Take Over Responsibilities

    You may need to find someone to watch your children, take care of your house, or do other jobs you are responsible for.

  2. 2

    Let Your Employer Know

    Tell them that medical travel has been recommended, and you may be going to Ottawa in the near future.

  3. 3

    Pack your luggage

    Scroll down for detailed information on what you should and shouldn't bring.

Do's and dont's to pack

Making a list of all the things you want to bring or take care of before you go may help you feel organized and less anxious.

Do Pack
Clothing & Outerwear, Medications, Healthcare Card, and One Piece of Photo ID to board planes.
Don't Pack
Ulu, and Liquids over 100 mL (You can't bring them as carry-on, but they can go in checked luggage.)

OHSNI (Ontario Ottawa Health Services Network Inc.)

OHSNI is a non-profit group that has managed health care for Baffin residents in Ottawa for over 20 years. They will walk you through the process of getting to your appointments. Visit their website for more detailed steps and contact information.
